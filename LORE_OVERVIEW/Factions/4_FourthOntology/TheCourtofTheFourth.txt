= Overview =
Composed of all humans beyond [[The 4th's Wall]], whether they're aware of it or not, '''The 4th Beyond''' are the scribes of fiction's fate. They are metaphysical beings that rest on a plane higher than the characters of these tales, each member has their own reason for meddling with the world of fantasy— from mere entertainment or self-fulfilment, or in search for a greater purpose or to push a deeper message.

As a member of The 4th Beyond, it is nigh critical for [[you]] to recognize your place in the fictional world, sooner or later. Acknowledge your capabilities with it, your influence beyond ontological scale, and the role you play in the narrative.

== Skribbles ==
[[File:SkribbleArt.png|left|thumb]]
Making up a vast majority of The Court's inventory, '''skribbles''' are the <u>primordial flickers of creativity</u>, birthed when humanity first dabbled with the concept of fiction, long before pens were around.

Manifesting as soul flickers of pale green flames and often taking on spirit-like appearances, these little fatelings are what you use to script the fate of characters in your stories.<br>When something bad happens to a character because you wrote it to happen, you wrote that thanks to your skribble. As the main character pushes forward against all odds, they persist due to your insistence, through your skribble. They are the '''bridge''' between your <u>creative will</u>, and their <u>fictional reality</u>.

However, due to the anciency of this concept, skribbles have gone through many reiterations, revisions, and improvements over the years.<br>Long ago, they were <u>a lot more creative</u> and carried much more <u>free will</u>, as humanity has yet to fully grasp the concept of creativity and what limits it lacks. These manifested in wild but authentic cave paintings glittered atop a firmament of stone, backlit merely by humanity's own flickering flame.<br>As society advanced, so did the skribbles.  

When hieroglyphics were being carved, their individual will expressed as unique iconography for every character in ancient human scripts. As pens were born, skribbles grew tails on their necks, mimicking our own quill tipped creations. However, once the printing press was invented, problems began to arise.  

Their individuality and free will started becoming a problem. Although the population of scribbles grew exponentially in relation to the number of creative humans in your world, that also carried a much wilder and less comprehensive mass of creativity. Letters were harder to print, ideas more difficult to comprehend. Then, this climaxed with the [[Farewell Avalon|Protos and Archie Incident]].  

Ever since then, Skribbles have been <u>mostly neutered of their own free will</u>, giving more focus to you writers instead.<br>Their right arms have been severed, fully symbolizing their position as your "left-hand men" in writing stories. Their voice, taken away and replaced with your own. Creativity, individuality, all depended on their owners. And although you may never feel them, they are always by your side.<br>Though they no longer have a voice, some believe that your "booms of inspiration" are the whispers of your skribble, doing its best to break through its silence to bring you back to creativity.  
== Skravatars ==
Although they carry similarities to Skribbles, Skravatars complete an entirely different function and purpose. Rather than acting as your pen to script fate, <u>skravatars carry your will into fiction</u>. Essentially, they are '''self-insert''' characters.

This is not to be mistaken with the typical fictional trope of having a bland and empty main character for the viewer/reader/player to project themselves. Instead, skravatars are '''entirely blank slates''', who's <u>appearance</u>, <u>backstory</u>, and <u>personality</u> are '''yours''' and entirely your own. They carry no free will whatsoever and are solely meant for you to interact with the fictional world yourself, without needing to use a skribble to puppeteer a character.

Although skravatars are mostly reserved for Fourth Marshal members, there are no requirements to you writing your own skravatar. While it is suggested they carry pale green color motifs to match with The Court, this is not a requirement either.

== The World Builder ==
She is your right-hand man in wiring stories. If Skribbles control the narrative, and Skravatars lets you playtest it, ''she'' lets you build the setting.

Just like skribbles, you use her whenever you build a new world. Although, direct visitations are strictly off-limits.
