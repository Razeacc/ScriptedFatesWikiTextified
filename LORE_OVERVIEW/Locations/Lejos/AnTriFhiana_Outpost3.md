Idea: The Largest of Avalon's outposts, eventually having become the last-known major Archkin refuge prior to The Spirepocalypse. They primarily focused on researching the Soul by studying souls within the large collection of timelines positioned high above it. This massive underground complex had sprawling tunnels connected via deep maglev systems. It even spanned across two continents via an undersea maglev.

# 📚 Leabhar Amphitheatre
-# (Stoa Memoria Sub-Section)

-# Fracturing Zone Coverage:
~12%
-# Role:
True archive of An Trí-Fhiana and gathering place for Ubisoft Collectibles

-# Function:
> - **Memory Pieces encrypted as melodies**
> - Avalonian resistance to Morgale’s direct control
> - **Psyche Heritage Recollection**

-# Mechanics:
> - Melodies found throughout An Trí-Fhiana
> - Singing melodies here reconstructs Memory Pieces from ambient matter (atoms) in the air
> - Over half of melodies remain locked, foreshadowing the second continent
> 
> - **Archose Heritage Retracing**: Crimmy can trace back the archose inside any magic-innate people to discover what locked memories are contained within them
> 
> - **Lost Crimmies**: Crimmy clones can be found all across The Continent. Fining and returning them to Crimmy here lets her do a better job at retracing heritage

-# Key Features:
> - Vast rows of seats and bookshelves, all empty.
> - Walking in, you might remember something if enough memory piece particles enters your eye, and by extension, your soul
> - A stage wth a bunch of archamachine automate instruments
> - Archose Heritage Retracing machine thingy, powered by Crimmy, behind it

-# Collectibles:
> **Lost Crimmies**- Upgrades Archose Heritage Retracing
> 
> **Memory Melodies** - Unlocks important Memory Pieces from before The Spirepocalypse
> 
-# Vibe:
> - Gradually fills with layered chorales as memories are restored (think of this as one of those "collectible gallary" areas in Ubisoft games that slowly fills with content as you find the game's collectibled scattered across the map)
> - The air is thick with dust and tiny memory piece particles
> - Walking in, you might remember something if enough memory piece particles enters your eye, and by extension, your soul
