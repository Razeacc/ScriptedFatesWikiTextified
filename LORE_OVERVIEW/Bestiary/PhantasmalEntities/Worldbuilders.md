## Chatty's Memory Notes
Worldbuilders are gigantic invisible entities associated with Ulhysse and large-scale reality construction.

Although many civilizations remain unaware of them, Worldbuilders inhabit worlds constantly, subtly shaping environments, structures, ecosystems, and conceptual growth over time according to narrative pressure and authorial will.

Most people never perceive them directly.

Their invisibility is believed to result from:
- conceptual scale mismatch
- narrative filtering
- subconscious exclusion
- protective cognition barriers

Worldbuilders effectively act as living creative infrastructure.

When active worlds are maintained through sufficient narrative attention and conceptual momentum, Worldbuilders continue functioning normally.

However, abandoned worlds cause catastrophic consequences.

When a world's narrative support collapses:
- Worldbuilders deteriorate
- conceptual integrity fails
- Raw Creativity leaks outward
- Decanoluzion Zones form

This leaking substance represents unstable unprocessed creative potential.

Inside Decanoluzion Zones:
- physics destabilizes
- narrative consistency weakens
- unfinished concepts manifest
- abandoned possibilities emerge
- reality behaves like incomplete fiction

Some researchers believe Failed Epicenters partially resemble Worldbuilders because they unsuccessfully synchronize with the same underlying creative architecture tied to Ulhysse.
