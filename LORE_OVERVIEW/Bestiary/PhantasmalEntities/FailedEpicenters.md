## Chatty's Memory Notes
Failed Epicenters are entities that unsuccessfully attempted conceptual ascension into Epicenterhood, specifically, for the Epicenter of Fiction.

Because Fiction inherently involves witnessing, interpretation, imagination, emotional projection, and authorship compatibility, candidates for the Epicenter of Fiction undergo a uniquely dangerous process.

Requirements include:
- conceptual compatibility
- narrative density
- emotional witness
- a fragment of Ulhysse
- successful completion of the Fiction Trial

Failure results in catastrophic conceptual destabilization.

The candidate partially loses stable individuality while gaining malformed narrative-authoring properties.

Most Failed Epicenters gradually transform into giant monstrous entities resembling Worldbuilders.

These forms are believed to result from:
- incomplete authorship synchronization
- uncontrolled narrative generation
- identity collapse
- conceptual overload

Failed Epicenters often display:
- unstable anatomy
- impossible geometry
- reality distortion
- narrative leakage
- spontaneous environmental rewriting
- compulsive "creation" behavior

Some continue attempting to "finish stories" despite lacking coherent understanding of what stories even are anymore.

The Epicenter of Fiction is considered the most dangerous Epicenter partly because failure itself creates existential threats.
