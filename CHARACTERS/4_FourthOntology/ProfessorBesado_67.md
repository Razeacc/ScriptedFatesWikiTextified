Born shortly after the Spirepocalypse wiped the memories of everyone on The Continent, Ciela Besado stands as an inspiring Scientific Leader for the survivors from the calamity.
Having a seemingly-inmate wealth of knowledge from the pre-spirepocalypse world, she’s been working mostly by herself to bring society back to the modern age.
However, Besado alone cannot accomplish such a feat.

When the Otherworlders began falling from The Spire 2 years ago, that’s when she knew:
Help had finally arrived.

In truth, her lineage (the Besado Lineage) began the Archgod Revolution with Ciela the 1st being "The Kissed one" who killed an archgod in defiance with the help of The 4th Beyond and 1st Beyond (Memetta). Ever since, Memetta has been tied as a "personal servant" to all future Besado daughters. Furthermore, all Ciela Besados slowly stop aging before the age of 24. They stop aging completely after that, until they give birth to a daughter. Once a daughter is born, the new mother passes her immortality onto the daughter as her body rapidly ages to its true age. This Besado is the 67th.

Detailed Info
Lifestyle
Besado is quite strained, despite her positive appearance.

She’d spend every early morning using her fresh charcoal to write “Science Pamphlets”, among other things..
After a morning meal, she’d walk to Newpoint Village and inspect the hot air balloon's progress while handing out pamphlets and boosting morale. People could notice a subtle red mark on a random part of her skin during this time, but it eventually fades as the day passes.

The professor is quite aware of the placebo effect, as well as how much an attitude can affect others. To maintain a positive atmosphere and work environment, she keeps up the confident and bubbly mask, occasionally slipping her true feelings out in the form of slightly unhinged comedic behavior.

After managing the project, she’d spend the evening walking back to San Baciato while collecting wood. Then, before she sleeps, she burns the wood into charcoal. Besado has a system where once the fire dies and the charcoal is ready, a pebble would unravel and drop on a random part of her body, waking her up no matter the time, and leaving a red mark somewhere on her sensitive skin. Then, the cycle repeats. With how intricate and fine-tuned her process is, it’s almost as if she’s been doing this her whole life.

She occasionally power-naps while working. To avoid deep sleep, she holds a pebble over a clay pot beside her chair. When she’d accidentally fall asleep, her grip on the stone would slip, breaking the pot, and waking her back up again.

Era of Silence

Arriving in San Baciato shortly after The Spirepocalypse, a group of Midwives cradled the newborn Besado in their arms as they joined the growing commune of other survivors. They claimed her mother died at birth, and they were merely her caretakers.
As Besado grew up, one by one, The Midwives vanished without a trace, not like they had much influence in the town anyways, constantly acting like ghosts and rarely communicating to the villagefolk. It didn’t help that they looked quite eerie and difficult to approach, with their horned red hoods and cold expressions.

Eventually, when they realized she was all alone, the villagers took her into their warm arms, raising her as their own. She was a scrawny, hyperactive, silly little thing, and she was loved for it.

The Kid Scientist
Age 12 - 16

Growing up in San Baciato, people began to notice Besado’s above-average intelligence. Stating, advising, and doing some things a lot of modern people would do.
While other young kids were playing with sticks and stones, she was building and pulling around kites.
As adults collected sticks around the nearby woods, she’d string together tools and chop wood.
While everyone feared the dark, Besado braved the night with a handhelled blaze.
She was exactly who they needed, someone with a naturally-gifted mind for discovery, innovation, creativity, and science.

Quickly, thanks to her natural intelligence, she even rose through the social hierarchy, becoming the village’s leader.
More survivors from the Spirepocalypse found their way to San Baciato. And eventually, more people showed up too, those who were a bit more… foreign.

Present Day

Winchester’s Epilogue
Age 17 

Eighteen years following The Spirepocalypse, the sky would be lit up once more by something new.
Every night, an occasional burst of light would fall from The Spire, similar to a shooting star. Although most fell into the deep oceans that surround The Continent, rarely, they fall near land.
One happened to crash deep in a nearby forest.

After investigating this herself, Besado found a person wearing unusual clothes. With an unfamiliar accent and even more foreign knowledge, the scientist could only think of a single word to call someone like this: An Otherworlder.

He called himself Winchester, and after some time, the two became good companions, with him almost seeing Besado as his daughter in a way.
Wanting some privacy, the scientist helped him by naming the forest he now resided in as “Dregwoods”, and warned villagers not to venture too deep, as there was nothing to see.

Age 19

Besado always gazed up at the sky whenever she had the time, watching birds fly by and observing the winds blow trees across the land.
One day, she noticed something peculiar. The wind patterns on The Continent almost always seemed to blow in the direction of The Spire, casting leaves off into the distant sea far beyond the shores of their continent.
That’s when a brilliant idea sparked in her mind.

Using their newfound resources and tools, The Scientist gathered her survivors and otherworlders together, focusing the Stone Society’s efforts on a new town overlooking the sea— constructed on a dramatic cliff protruding into the deep unknown.

Personality:
On the outside, she’s very bubbly and positive, yapping about science and fun facts to keep people’s moods up while teaching things in an exciting manner.

But alone, Besado gets highly calculative and proficient with her work, taking on every project she’s the highest level of seriousness and dedication possible.
Although a bit of an overworker, with the fate of society on her shoulders, she feels there’s no other way out.


Appearance:
She wears a simple long-sleeved woven top made from hemp cloth and dyed with charcoal powder. The formula “A2 + B2 = C2” can be seen written near her collar using dried blood— the Pythagorean theorem.
Her pants are also made of hemp cloth, just like all the other fabrics made and used by the villagers. But unlike her top, this remains undyed.

A belt is strawn across her ways, carrying a pouch for soft carryables and a small tanned anime-hide pocket for sharper collectibles.
More hemp cloth is tied around her feet to act as socks, with simple wooden paddings serving as shoes.

Aside from clothing, her pure-white hair drapes down to the back of her thighs, it seems she hasn’t had time to trim it. And accompanying her soft round face, is a pair of inquisitive and glittering yellow eyes, always gazing upon the world in deep wonder.
